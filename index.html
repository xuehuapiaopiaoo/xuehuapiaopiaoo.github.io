Projects

"Plagar" "The Bedridder" "Salinity Sensor"
Plagar is a concept that my team and I came up with after defining a problem statement of waste. COVID-19 has hit everyone hard, and food delivery companies saw their stock rise dramatically, along with the plastic food containers that came with it. Thus, we decided to make an all-natural, fully biodegradable container made out of a key ingredient: Agar. Link
The BedRidder is an autonomous robot that eases the workload of nurses. The COVID-19 pandemic has caused hospitals all over the world to fill up at an alarming rate, causing many healthcare workeds to be overworked to the point of quitting. The BedRidder was then developed as a concept to take over the the nurse's role of moving patients back and forth. IR and Ultrasound sensors were introduced for the navigation, and a human-computer-interface was also developed for the nurses to direct the BedRidder. Link
The Salinity Sensor is an idea derived from the need to fulfill our project requirement: to make use of electromagnetics for sensing. Our group saw an opportunity in food stability in the form of fish farming, where prerequisites have to be met for optimal growth. We wanted a product that could do more than just that however, so we set out to design a product that could completely automate the salinity of a body of water using a SBC. Link
